How Results are calculated

The entire gene expression matrix (Cytokine/Control treated) is used as input for GSEA along with a phenotype class file, specifying the total number of classes - ARIA_CD362 - , ARIA_CD362 +, PA, TYTO. Before running GSEA, one must specify the phenotype comparison e.g ARIA_CD362 vs PA.

Step 1:

GSEA creates a list ranking each genes correlation with the specified phenotype using the signal to noise metric. Signal to noise calculates the difference between the mean values for a gene across both phenotype samples, and scales them by the sum of standard deviations. This means that genes with large differences in expression level between phenotypes and little variation between replicates are highly ranked.

Figure 1: Ranking input genes according to their Signal to Noise ratio.

Step 2:

User selects a set of pathways they are interested in. Gene sets are downloaded from https://www.gsea-msigdb.org/gsea/msigdb/index.jsp. For this analysis Immunologic, GO and Curated gene sets were used. The gene sets are defined as .

Step 3:

GSEA determines whether the members of are randomly distributed throughout or found at the top or bottom. This is denoted by an enrichment score, calculated by “walking” down the ranked list increasing a running-sum statistic when a gene is in the gene set and decreasing it when it is not. The magnitude of the increment depends on the correlation of the gene with a phenotype.

Positive ES indicates members of gene set are found at the top of the ranked list , whilst a negative ES score indicates enrichment at the lower half of , in genes that have been ranked lowly due to their poor correlation with the phenotype.

 

Step 4:

The ES values are normalised based on gene set size and then a false discovery rate is calculated, to give an estimated probability of false positives.
